Financial Aid

Need to know how to get money for college? Check this page out.

What is Financial Aid?

Financial Aid is when a university or higher-level institution gives you a scholarship. It's essentially money to help pay for your school. Students don't have to pay them back, and they come out of merit, or out of a need-based situation.

What is Need-Based Financial Aid?

Need Based Financial Aid is when a school believes that you will need financial aid based on your family's gross income. Often times, schools are also Need-Blind: this means that they won't look at whether you need money based on your family's financial situation, but will be purely based on merit and your abilities as a student.

For Financial Aid, you'll want to keep a close eye on the FAFSA, or the "Free Application for Federal Student Aid". The website is shown below. You'll want to make sure this form is 100% Accurate, and make sure to do it with a parent or legal guardian.




Also, you'll want to make sure to find out if you are eligible for a Fee Waiver. Fee Waivers will often give you a free admission fee for a college, voiding the $60-80 fee for applying to colleges.





You'll also want to find out about whether or not your school offers scholarships. Often times, schools will offer a small chunk of money for scholarships based on your contribution to the school as a student, be it in leadership roles or just helping out. Normally students receive this for working very hard in school, keeping their grades up, and also committing themselves to their high school. If you're a low income family, school and working your way to a potential college scholarship should be a high priority for you.
